    1. Overview ¬∂

    Royal Mail of Vekllei is representative of the country itself. For many foreigners, Royal Mail aircraft may be the closest they ever come to seeing the mysterious Atlantic country, and for residents the ancient organisation is a living relic of Vekllei’s ancient history, and an ongoing testament to the longevity of their people.

    T he Royal Mail of Vekllei, referred to as the Mail indigenously, is the national postal carrier of Vekllei. It is a military state requisite reponsible for all postage in the country, and delivers nearly 50 million mail pieces domestically and abroad each day. One of the oldest institutions of its kind in Vekllei, it was founded in 1422 as a courier system between the Vekllei Kingdom and its largely mercenary military force. In 1742, it was formalised as a modern postal system available to the civilian public, and today offers a variety of services available to all Vekllei residents, including external territories.

    Systems of Language

    3. Rapotenne, is the traditional logographic language of names. It is much older than Topet, and has more in common with spiritual runes than modern logography.

    History ¬∂

    Although Vekllei has maintained courier systems for at least a thousand years, the Mail as a contiguous instituion was founded in 1422 by the Desimou VII, King of Vekllei, in the late Period of Rest. During this time, the lords of Vekllei has established significant mercantile holdings across the North Atlantic, and had developed reliable systems of trade throughout Ireland and Denmark. The Royal Mail of Vekllei was first established as a royal communications organ that sought to ensure stability of the Desimou rule across the expanding, and increasingly autonomous, Vekllei empire. In its original form, it consisted of a handful of messengers attached to the feudal military apparatus of the Vekllei monarchy, and upon the collapse of the kingdom regular operations transferred to the incoming Junta-backed Vekllei Republic.

    The Mail would not come to resemble conventional civilian Mail until 1652, when the Junta expanded postal contracts to the privy councils of the Faroe and Greenland territories, and later broke apart the Mail monopoly to benefit then-Secretary of State Kism Sismiosnah, who established the New Parliament Postal Service.

    Structure ¬∂

    The Mail is organised horizontally as an independent branch of the Royal Vekllei Armed Forces, and so, unlike other State Requisites, technically reports directly to the Prime Ministers of the Vekllei Government, as representatives of the Landscape Sovereign. Like most large-scale industry in Vekllei, the Mail is vertically integrated, and most of its contract work is consolidated directly into the bureau structure.

    2. Services ¬∂

    The Royal Mail maintains a constitutionally-bound monopoly on all postage in the country, leaving it the sole provider of universal postage service in Vekllei. Like most State Requisites (A.r.), however, it has substantial international trade and foreign dealings that provide for its common description as a state-owned for-profit enterprise, or public corporation.

    U niversal service is, by the glossary of the Mail, truly universal. As mandated by Vekllei law, the Mail provides delivery for all postage material anywhere in the world, or indeed, beyond it. It maintains 6,424 post offices across Vekllei, including its antarctic and peripheral possessions. The Mail’s first Lunar Post Office opened in 2059. In addition to its domestic monopoly, the Mail contributes nearly VK‚ù¶2.6 trillion each year towards the Vekllei Government Securities Bureau (the country’s sovereign wealth fund) through its international postage and courier services abroad, under the name World Post.

    Since the Mail’s Office of Telecommunications is also responsible for all telephones, telegrams and videophones in the country, the Mail’s status as a lynchpin of communication is absolute, and heavily protected by the postwar Vekllei constitution.

    Foreign Service ¬∂

    Foreign service refers to any work performed by the World Post for international clients, which has established itself as one of the Big Four international transnational courier services across the world today. Similar to domestic postal systems, the World Post has flat rates for delivery anywhere in the world, priced according to size and weight.
