    Decentralisation and integration are the foundations of Sundress Municipalism, as core to life experience in the country as moneylessness or petticoatism.

    Property ownership is universal in Vekllei, if obscured by the recurring contradictions of landscape-as-being:

    All personal land in Vekllei is ostensibly private, with a single ancient landlord; the place itself, with interests and heritage tacked to it by Upen’s sense of place. In practice, ownership is de facto private, but is also portioned between players and brokers in the community, including neighbours and tenant unions. This means that most land is determined by precedent, which reigns supreme in Vekllei’s postwar constitution.
